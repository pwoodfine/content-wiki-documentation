# TOPIC-MEMO-01: Institutional Communications Doctrine

## 1. The CapEx Posture (Lexicon & Tone)
* **Mandate:** All narrative must align with physical real estate and infrastructure CapEx. Explicitly reject software-as-a-service (SaaS) styling.
* **Rule of Absolute Factuality:** Remove all subjective qualifiers and adjectives. State exactly what the system does or who the customer is without embellishment.
* **Zero-Gimmick Transitions:** Do not use marketing hooks or sales transitions. Rely entirely on structural causality.

## 2. Regulatory Compliance (The BCSC Shield)
* **The Anti-Puffery Verb Doctrine:** For technology in active engineering or deployment, forbid absolute, present-tense accomplishment verbs to prevent misrepresentation under BCSC/SEC guidelines. Use "is engineered to," "is actively deploying," or "is architected to constitute."
* **Entity Precision:** Never guess corporate designations. You must strictly use the statutory definitions defined in the `GLOSSARY.md`.
* **Unincorporated Entities:** Never append statutory suffixes (e.g., AG, Inc., LLC) to an entity prior to final legal incorporation.

## 3. Jurisdictional Agnosticism
* **The Translation Mandate:** Abstract all localized legal, identity, and property structures into universal terms to prevent Jurisdictional Fracture during international translation. You must use the universal abstractions defined in the `GLOSSARY.md`.

## 4. Intellectual Property (The Trademark Doctrine)
* **The First-Use Rule:** Append the ™ symbol *only* to the first prominent use (headers) and the first use in the body text. Do not clutter the document with redundant symbols.
* **Defensive Publication:** To establish patent-blocking prior art, external communications must explicitly name the underlying architectural mechanics (e.g., microkernel, authorization protocols) rather than just the user-facing features.

## 5. Structural Headers
* **Mandate:** Headers must act as definitive, executive summaries.
* **Format:** Use two-to-three-word noun phrases that establish the core thesis (e.g., "Structural Ownership.").
